Untitled
And I used to think my worst nights were the ones with tears but I was wrong.

The most dreadful nights are, those when you feel nothing at all. You remain unmoved from the spot, where you've sat for hours on end. No smile breaks through, no comfort to send. Nothing stirs, no change in sight, In the void of nothingness, I lose the fight. I used to feel everything, every sway, Now, in emptiness, I merely stay.
— Hashim Malik
Misery — The Key
What if the key to all the pain The parts that yearn and remain Lies in doing all the things held back Thinking that the ability is what I lack?
What if the key to all the pain Is to let go of patterns that I did again Fearing the potential that lay within Dreaming of just what might have been
What if the key to all the pain Was trying not to do it again? Second guess & criticize every action Rewarding instead of punishing a reaction?
What if the key to all the pain Lies in learning not to complain To hide, constrain or defend but loving myself as my best friend. So my MISERY remain.
— Hashim Reyaz Malik
Misery — Guilt Within
The guilt you hold within, for me, Because my thoughts linger, you hauntingly. It torments me still, a relentless foe, For I see myself as hideous now, I know.
The power behind you, Depriving you from the warmth of my heart. No more remonstrance, no complaints I bear, For now, I understand, I am aware.
In silence, I embrace the guilt, let it be your guide, While I hide my sorrow deep inside.
Quiesce for wounds unseen, In this quiet torment, I glean.
I'm misery, unseen unbound None can see me, none around.
— Hashim Malik

Self-repair  (Misery)
It's funny how one day you look back at your life and you realize that you finally moved on. And it didn't come out of the blue, but one day it clicks in that your infatuation is gone. And you didn't necessarily find anything new. It took a lot of work, but you finally chose you. You finally asked yourself out for a dance. You finally gave healing a chance. How one day you look back at your history and you realize that you finally moved on from the misery.
— Hashim Malik

My lungs have not taken a full breath since you left. Because without you, the oxygen has exited the room. And I've held hands too. But my palms still feel empty because none of them feel like you. My mind has become a tape recorder that is stuck on a constant loop. Replying the sound of your laughter. It is like a catchy but sad tune. And I often wonder, if in your mind my voice is playing too. I stare at the ceiling of my room and replay the last time we spoke. When no one else is watching. And I wish it hadn't ended so soon. Tell me — is it easier to breathe now that I've disappeared, or are you suffocating too?
— Hashim Malik

I'm Paradoxical
I like to be happy, but I think about sad things all the time.
I don't really like myself, but I love the person I've become.
I say I don't care, but I just care too much, deep into my bones.
I crave attention, yet I reject everything that comes my way.
I heal people, but I broke my own heart trying to fix them.
I love to listen, but I never tell them what's inside me.

Tell me — do you understand my misery?
— Hashim Malik

Untitled Reflection
Why don't I accept my fate, even though I can see it clearly? Yet my heart can't seem to comprehend. Why do I run after something that isn't mine? Why do I keep forgetting that what I wish for has gone down the hill? When I can see clearly that there is no way to go there, why do I keep looking beyond the horizon, where I see nothing but misery?

I guess it's the intellectual me and the emotional me battling within. But in the end, no matter who wins or loses, the whole of me will always be a loser. I end up feeling like a ne'er-do-well.
— Hashim Malik
